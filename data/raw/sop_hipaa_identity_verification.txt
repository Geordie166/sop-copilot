STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Document ID: SOP-CC-001
Title: Member Identity Verification Prior to Discussing Protected Health Information
Effective Date: 2026-01-05
Revision: 4
Department: Member Services Call Center

PURPOSE
This procedure defines the mandatory steps a service representative must
complete to verify a caller's identity before discussing any protected
health information (PHI), including eligibility, claims, benefits, or
prior authorization status. It exists to prevent unauthorized disclosure
of member health information.

SCOPE
Applies to all Member Services representatives handling inbound and
outbound calls related to member accounts.

PROCEDURE
1. Greet the caller and ask them to state their full name and date of
   birth.
2. Ask the caller to provide their member ID number as printed on their
   health plan ID card.
3. Cross-check the name, date of birth, and member ID against the record
   in the member management system; all three must match exactly.
4. If the caller is not the member (e.g., spouse, parent of a minor
   dependent, or authorized representative), confirm the caller's
   relationship to the member and verify an active authorized
   representative flag exists on the account.
5. If no authorized representative flag exists, inform the caller that
   you cannot discuss PHI with them and offer to send an authorization
   form.
6. If verification succeeds, proceed with the call and document in the
   call notes that identity was verified, including the method used.
7. If verification fails after two attempts, do not disclose any PHI.
   Offer to have the member call back directly or request written
   authorization.
8. For calls transferred from another department, re-verify identity;
   verification does not carry over between departments.

EXCEPTIONS
Calls from providers' offices calling on behalf of a member follow a
separate provider verification procedure (SOP-CC-004) and are not covered
by this document.

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
Every call touching PHI must include a verification status note in the
call record before any account details are discussed, per company policy
and applicable privacy regulations.
