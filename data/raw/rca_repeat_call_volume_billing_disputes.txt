ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS REPORT

Investigation ID: RCA-2026-0126
Title: Repeat-Call Volume for Unresolved Billing Dispute Issues
Date Range Investigated: 2026-04-01 to 2026-04-30
Investigated By: P. Alvarado, Quality Analyst

PROBLEM STATEMENT
Members who called about a premium billing dispute called back a second
time on the same issue within 14 days at a rate of 34 percent, well above
the center's first-call resolution target repeat-call threshold of 12
percent, indicating that initial resolutions were not sticking.

DATA REVIEWED
Repeat-call reports linked by member ID and reason code, a sample of 35
paired initial-and-repeat call recordings, and the billing adjustment
processing timeline.

FINDINGS
In nearly all sampled repeat calls, the representative on the initial call
told the member the adjustment was "resolved" and would appear on their
next statement, but the adjustment had only been submitted to a separate
billing department queue with a processing time of 5 to 10 business days.
Members interpreted "resolved" as immediate and called back when no change
appeared within a few days.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (5 WHYS)
1. Why do members call back? They see no change to their bill after being
   told the issue was resolved.
2. Why do they expect an immediate change? Representatives use the word
   "resolved" without specifying that the adjustment is pending downstream
   processing.
3. Why do representatives phrase it that way? There is no standard
   scripting for communicating pending versus completed adjustment status.
4. Why is there no standard scripting? The call script library was last
   updated before the separate billing adjustment queue was introduced.
5. Why wasn't the script library updated at that time? Script updates are
   only triggered by direct escalation requests, and this gap was never
   escalated.

ROOT CAUSE
Representatives lack standardized language to distinguish a submitted
adjustment from a completed one, causing members to believe billing issues
are fixed immediately when they are actually pending a 5 to 10 business day
downstream process.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
1. Update the call script library with required language distinguishing
   "submitted for adjustment" from "resolved" (Owner: Quality Assurance,
   Due: 2026-05-20).
2. Add an automated text or email confirmation when a billing adjustment
   is submitted, including the expected processing window (Owner: IT
   Systems, Due: 2026-06-15).
3. Trigger a script library review whenever a new downstream processing
   queue is introduced (Owner: Quality Assurance, Due: 2026-05-01).
