STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Document ID: SOP-CC-003
Title: After-Call Work Documentation Standards
Effective Date: 2026-01-20
Revision: 3
Department: Member Services Call Center

PURPOSE
This procedure defines the minimum documentation standard representatives
must complete during after-call work (ACW) following every member call. It
exists to ensure accurate, auditable records of member interactions and to
support continuity when a member calls back about the same issue.

SCOPE
Applies to all Member Services representatives for every completed call,
including calls that were transferred or escalated.

PROCEDURE
1. Select the correct call reason code from the standardized list; do not
   use the generic "Other" code unless no listed reason applies.
2. Record the identity verification method used, per SOP-CC-001.
3. Summarize the member's request in plain language, avoiding
   abbreviations that are not on the approved abbreviation list.
4. Document each action taken during the call, including any systems
   updated, forms submitted, or transfers made.
5. Record the outcome of the call: resolved, pending follow-up, or
   escalated.
6. If the outcome is pending follow-up, enter a specific follow-up date
   and the owning department.
7. Complete ACW within the target of 90 seconds after call disconnect to
   maintain queue availability, without sacrificing completeness.
8. Submit the call record before accepting the next call.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS
Call notes are subject to random quality audit. Notes must be specific
enough that another representative could understand the member's issue
and prior actions without needing to listen to the call recording.

COMMON ERRORS TO AVOID
Do not copy and paste identical notes across multiple calls from the same
member without updating call-specific details. Do not leave the outcome
field blank. Do not use member-identifying details in the free-text reason
field beyond what is necessary for context.
