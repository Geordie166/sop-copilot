ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS REPORT

Investigation ID: RCA-2026-0112
Title: Elevated Average Handle Time on Prior Authorization Status Calls
Date Range Investigated: 2026-03-01 to 2026-03-31
Investigated By: S. Okonkwo, Quality Analyst

PROBLEM STATEMENT
Average handle time (AHT) for calls tagged with reason code "Prior
Authorization Status" rose from a baseline of 6 minutes 40 seconds to 11
minutes 15 seconds over the reporting month, exceeding the department
target of 7 minutes and contributing to increased queue wait times
center-wide.

DATA REVIEWED
Call reason code volume reports, a sample of 40 recorded calls, and
representative interviews from three shifts.

FINDINGS
Recorded calls showed representatives frequently placing members on hold
for 3 to 5 minutes while manually checking prior authorization status in a
separate legacy system that does not share a login session with the
primary call platform, requiring representatives to re-enter member ID and
date of birth a second time.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (5 WHYS)
1. Why is AHT elevated on these calls? Representatives spend several
   minutes on hold looking up status manually.
2. Why is the lookup manual? The legacy prior authorization system was not
   integrated with the primary call platform during the last system
   upgrade.
3. Why wasn't it integrated? The integration was deprioritized in favor of
   claims-system integration, which had a higher volume of call types at
   the time.
4. Why has this not been revisited since? No formal process exists to
   re-evaluate deprioritized integration items after go-live.
5. Why is there no re-evaluation process? Post-launch system reviews focus
   on defect tracking, not on deferred scope items.

ROOT CAUSE
The prior authorization lookup system was never integrated with the
primary call platform, forcing a manual, duplicate-entry lookup that adds
several minutes to every call of this type.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
1. Submit an integration request to IT to add single sign-on lookup for
   prior authorization status within the primary call platform (Owner: IT
   Systems, Due: 2026-06-01).
2. In the interim, provide representatives a saved-session shortcut to
   reduce manual entry (Owner: Call Center Operations, Due: 2026-04-15).
3. Add a formal deferred-scope review step to future system launch
   checklists (Owner: Quality Assurance, Due: 2026-05-01).
