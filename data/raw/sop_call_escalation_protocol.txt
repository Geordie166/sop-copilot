STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

Document ID: SOP-CC-002
Title: Call Escalation Protocol for Member Services
Effective Date: 2026-02-01
Revision: 2
Department: Member Services Call Center

PURPOSE
This procedure defines when and how a representative must escalate a call
to a Tier 2 specialist or supervisor. It exists to ensure complex or
sensitive issues reach staff with the appropriate authority and expertise,
and to keep escalation handling consistent across the center.

SCOPE
Applies to all Tier 1 Member Services representatives.

ESCALATION TRIGGERS
Escalate a call immediately when any of the following occur: the member
requests a supervisor by name or title; the issue involves a grievance or
appeal; the call involves a threat of self-harm or harm to others; the
resolution requires an account adjustment exceeding the representative's
authorization limit of 500 dollars; or the member has called three or
more times about the same unresolved issue.

PROCEDURE
1. Inform the member that you will be transferring them to a specialist
   who can better assist with their request.
2. Place the member on hold using the warm-transfer hold, not a blind
   transfer.
3. Contact the Tier 2 queue and provide a verbal summary including the
   member's verified identity status, the issue, and any actions already
   taken.
4. Confirm the Tier 2 specialist is ready before connecting the member.
5. Remain on the line for the introduction, then disconnect once the
   specialist confirms they have the details.
6. Log the escalation reason and receiving specialist's name in the call
   record.
7. If no Tier 2 specialist is available within 3 minutes, offer the
   member a scheduled callback within 24 hours rather than extending hold
   time indefinitely.

SUPERVISOR ESCALATIONS
Requests for a supervisor must be honored within one transfer attempt.
Representatives may not attempt to resolve the issue themselves after a
named supervisor request has been made, as this can be perceived as
withholding access.

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
All escalations must be tagged with an escalation reason code in the call
record for quality reporting.
